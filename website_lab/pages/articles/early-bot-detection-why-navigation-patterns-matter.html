Early Bot Detection: Why Navigation Patterns Matter

Detecting scraping behavior early can reduce data leakage and improve defensive response time.

Traditional defenses frequently rely on thresholds or static fingerprints.

However, modern scrapers can rotate user agents, slow down request timing, and imitate browsers.

Navigation behavior remains harder to fake consistently because it reflects how a client moves through the site graph over time.

Humans often pause, compare, revisit, and branch unpredictably.

Bots tend to exhibit more structured traversals, whether breadth-first, depth-first, or inventory-like page enumeration.

This difference can be translated into graph metrics and entropy-based features.

A strong research contribution is not just detecting bots accurately, but detecting them early.

Measurements such as requests-to-detection and time-to-detection make the evaluation much more practical.
