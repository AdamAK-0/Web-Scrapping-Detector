Invisible Honeypots in Modern Web Applications

Hidden endpoints and decoy paths can provide high-confidence evidence of automation.

Honeypots are especially attractive because they can provide near-certain evidence of automation when designed carefully.

A hidden link that is not visible or reachable to normal users but is present in the DOM may still be discovered by simplistic crawlers.

Modern honeypots do not need to be obvious.

They can appear as hidden offers, undocumented diagnostic pages, or decoy API patterns.

The challenge is balancing detection value with usability and avoiding search-engine indexing or accidental triggering by assistive tooling.

For research environments, honeypots are best used as a labeled signal rather than the only defense.

They can enrich training data and provide a useful auxiliary feature in a larger behavioral detection pipeline.
