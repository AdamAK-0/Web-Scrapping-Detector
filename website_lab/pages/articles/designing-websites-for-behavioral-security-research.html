Designing Websites for Behavioral Security Research

How structured navigation, categories, and controlled page depth can support scraper-detection experiments.

Behavior-based bot detection benefits from websites that contain diverse but realistic navigation flows.

A useful research site should include hubs, leaf pages, category pages, content pages, and functional endpoints such as search or contact forms.

This variety creates richer traversal graphs and makes human navigation measurably different from scripted scraping.

When designing a site for security research, it is also important to keep the content believable.

Participants browse more naturally when the interface feels polished and the categories make sense.

A clean e-commerce or product showcase website is often a strong choice because it contains repeated templates, product detail pages, and category transitions.

Finally, a research website should support logging at the HTTP layer.

Access logs, referrers, paths, timestamps, and user-agent strings become essential when building real-world detection pipelines.
